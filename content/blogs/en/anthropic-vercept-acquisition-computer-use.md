# Anthropic Acquires Vercept: A Strategic Move That Reshapes the Computer Use Race

**TL;DR:** Anthropic's acquisition of Vercept brings a team of Allen Institute for AI alumni with deep computer vision expertise, accelerating Claude's computer use capabilities to 72.5% on the OSWorld benchmark—up from under 15% in late 2024—while sending UiPath stock down 3.6% as traditional RPA vendors face mounting pressure from AI-native automation.

## Background: The Race for AI That Uses Computers

The AI industry's next frontier isn't just chatbots that answer questions—it's agents that can actually operate software the way humans do. Click buttons. Navigate spreadsheets. Fill out forms across multiple browser tabs. Complete multi-step workflows without requiring custom API integrations or pre-written scripts.

This capability, generally called "computer use," represents a fundamental shift in how AI interacts with the digital world. Instead of requiring developers to build bespoke integrations for every application, computer use agents perceive screens visually and interact through simulated mouse clicks and keyboard inputs, just like a human operator would.

Anthropic introduced Claude's computer use capabilities in October 2024 with Claude 3.5 Sonnet, which scored 14.9% on the OSWorld benchmark—a test suite that presents hundreds of tasks across real software like Chrome, LibreOffice, and VS Code running on simulated computers. That score was roughly double the next-best system at the time, but still far from practically useful.

The benchmark progression since then tells a story of rapid improvement: 28.0% with Sonnet 3.5 v2, 42.2% with Sonnet 3.6, 61.4% with Sonnet 4.5, and now [72.5% with Sonnet 4.6](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-sonnet-4-6), released on February 17, 2026. That's a nearly five-fold improvement in 16 months.

## What Happened: The Acquisition Details

On February 25, 2026, Anthropic announced it had [acquired Vercept](https://www.anthropic.com/news/acquires-vercept), a Seattle-based AI startup that built Vy, a desktop application enabling AI-driven task automation through computer vision. The acquisition brings Vercept's nine-person team—including co-founders Kiana Ehsani, Luca Weihs, and Ross Girshick—under Anthropic's roof.

The founding team reads like an all-star roster from Seattle's AI research scene. Ehsani, Vercept's CEO, spent 10 years working on embodied AI and robotics, leading the embodied AI team at the Allen Institute for AI (Ai2) before founding Vercept. Weihs, a former Ai2 research manager, focused on AI agents and reinforcement learning. Girshick is a computer vision pioneer who also spent time at Meta AI.

Vercept had raised approximately $50 million, including a $16 million seed round in January 2025 that valued the company at $67 million post-money. The investor list included Eric Schmidt (former Google CEO), Jeff Dean (Google DeepMind Chief Scientist), and the AI2 Incubator, the startup accelerator affiliated with the Allen Institute. Financial terms of the Anthropic acquisition were not disclosed.

Vercept's product, Vy, will shut down within 30 days, with the company directing users to Claude as an alternative. This marks Anthropic's second acquisition, following its December 2025 purchase of Bun, the JavaScript runtime.

## Analysis: Why This Acquisition Makes Strategic Sense

### The Technical Gap Vercept Fills

Anthropic's announcement emphasized that Vercept's team possesses expertise in solving "hard perception and interaction problems" that code alone cannot address. This points to a specific technical challenge: while large language models excel at reasoning about what to do, actually perceiving and interacting with graphical user interfaces remains difficult.

Vercept's approach differed from traditional robotic process automation (RPA) tools like UiPath, which rely on APIs, screen element selectors, and pre-written scripts. Vy used computer vision to "see" and interpret screens the way humans do, enabling more flexible automation that doesn't break when an application's interface changes slightly.

The company claimed [92% accuracy on computer automation benchmarks](https://www.together.ai/customers/vercept) in partnership with Together AI, alongside 5x performance gains and 30% cost savings compared to alternative approaches. Even accounting for benchmark-specific optimization, these numbers suggest Vercept's team developed meaningful improvements in perception-action loops.

### Competitive Positioning

The acquisition comes as competition in the computer use space intensifies. OpenAI's [Operator](https://openai.com/index/introducing-operator/), launched in January 2025, achieved 38.1% on OSWorld when introduced and has since been integrated into ChatGPT as "agent mode." Microsoft has deployed specialized Copilot agents across Office applications.

But Anthropic's recent benchmarks suggest Claude has pulled ahead. With Sonnet 4.6 at 72.5% on OSWorld and Opus 4.6 at 72.7%, Anthropic claims Claude is "approaching human-level performance on tasks such as navigating complex spreadsheets and completing web forms across browser tabs."

Acquiring Vercept's expertise could help Anthropic maintain this lead. The team's background in embodied AI—training agents that can see, learn from, and interact with their surroundings—applies directly to the computer use problem.

### The Enterprise Play

Anthropic recently launched Claude Cowork, a desktop application for enterprise users. The Vercept acquisition strengthens this product's underlying capabilities, positioning Claude not just as a conversational AI but as a practical automation tool that can handle repetitive workflows.

This matters because enterprise buyers increasingly want AI that does things, not just AI that suggests things. A model that can actually navigate a CRM, extract data from invoices, or complete multi-step approval workflows has clear ROI in ways that conversational AI sometimes struggles to demonstrate.

## Industry Impact: RPA Vendors Take Notice

The market reaction to the acquisition was telling. [UiPath stock fell 3.6%](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/uipath-stock-falls-amid-anthropics-vercept-acquisition-news-93CH-4525267) on the news, extending a period of weakness that has seen the RPA leader decline amid concerns about AI encroaching on its territory.

The threat to traditional RPA vendors is structural. RPA tools require significant implementation work—mapping out workflows, building selectors to identify screen elements, handling exceptions when interfaces change. An AI system that can visually understand screens and adapt to interface changes without explicit programming undermines RPA's value proposition.

This doesn't mean UiPath and its competitors will disappear overnight. Enterprise customers have significant investments in existing RPA infrastructure, and regulated industries often prefer deterministic, auditable automation over probabilistic AI systems. But the competitive dynamics have shifted. RPA vendors now need to integrate AI capabilities rapidly or risk being disrupted by AI-native alternatives.

The broader automation software market—valued at over $10 billion annually—faces similar pressure. Any tool that automates workflows through screen interaction is now competing with AI systems that can perceive and act on screens directly.

## What's Next: The Path Forward

Several developments likely follow from this acquisition:

**Near-term integration**: Expect Vercept's perception and interaction improvements to appear in Claude within months. Anthropic's track record suggests rapid integration of acquired capabilities, as seen with Bun's technology influencing Claude Code and other developer tools.

**Expanded enterprise offerings**: Claude Cowork and related enterprise products will likely gain more sophisticated automation capabilities, positioning Anthropic to compete directly in the enterprise automation market.

**Benchmark progression continues**: If the OSWorld trajectory holds, Claude could reach 80%+ accuracy on computer use tasks by mid-2026, making AI-driven automation practical for a wider range of enterprise workflows.

**Competitive response**: OpenAI, Google, and Microsoft will accelerate their own computer use capabilities. The acquisition signals that specialized talent in perception and interaction is valuable, potentially triggering more acqui-hires in this space.

**RPA adaptation**: Traditional RPA vendors will likely accelerate AI integration, either through internal development, partnerships with AI providers, or acquisitions of their own. Survival requires becoming AI-native rather than AI-adjacent.

The Vercept acquisition is one move in a longer game. The ultimate prize is an AI system that can use any software, navigate any interface, and complete any digital task a human could—without requiring custom integrations or explicit programming. Anthropic has now assembled a team that believes it can solve that problem. The next 12-18 months will reveal whether they're right.
