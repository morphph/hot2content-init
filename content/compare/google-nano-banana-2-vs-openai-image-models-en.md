## Quick Verdict

Google's Nano Banana 2 (launched February 26, 2026) delivers 4K resolution at sub-10-second speeds with pricing roughly 50% cheaper than its predecessor. OpenAI's GPT-4o image generation offers superior text rendering and conversational refinement but costs more per image. Nano Banana 2 wins on speed, resolution, and price; GPT-4o wins on text accuracy and iterative editing workflows.

## Feature Deep Dive

### Resolution and Output Quality

Nano Banana 2's headline feature is 4K output—a ceiling that no competitor matches at consumer pricing. The model supports flexible aspect ratios from 512px to 4096px, making it suitable for everything from social media thumbnails to print-ready marketing materials.

GPT-4o maxes out at 1024x1024 for its highest quality setting. For workflows requiring 4K assets, this limitation forces users toward upscaling tools or alternative models.

### Text Rendering

GPT-4o's text rendering remains the industry standard. The omnimodel architecture handles complex typography, multi-language text, and precise placement with near-perfect accuracy. Marketing teams generating product mockups or social media graphics with text overlays will find GPT-4o more reliable.

Nano Banana 2 has improved text capabilities but still produces occasional misalignments, gibberish characters, or legibility issues—particularly with dense text or non-Latin scripts.

### Speed and Throughput

Nano Banana 2 generates images in 4-6 seconds, roughly 2-3x faster than GPT-4o's 10-20 second range. For batch workflows or real-time applications, this speed advantage compounds significantly.

### Subject Consistency

Both models support multi-subject consistency, but with different strengths. Nano Banana 2 maintains resemblance for up to 5 characters and 14 objects across a workflow—useful for storyboarding or brand asset creation. GPT-4o handles 10-20 distinct objects but struggles more with maintaining consistent human faces across multiple generations.

### Photorealism

Third-party benchmarks consistently rank Nano Banana higher for photorealistic human portraits. The model produces cleaner skin textures, more natural lighting, and better adherence to scene constraints. GPT-4o occasionally introduces subtle distortions in facial structure when editing or transforming uploaded images.

## Limitations and Safety Restrictions

### Nano Banana 2 Limitations

- **Overzealous safety filters**: Google has acknowledged that filters "became way more cautious than we intended," blocking legitimate prompts like "dog" or "bowl of cereal"
- **Watermarking**: All outputs include visible watermarks plus invisible SynthID marks—no clean commercial outputs without disclosure
- **Usage quotas**: Free tier limited to 2 images/day; Pro subscribers (~$20/month) get ~100/day
- **Fine detail editing**: Struggles with small objects in complex scenes or precise label/reflection edits

### GPT-4o Limitations

- **Resolution ceiling**: No 4K support at any tier
- **Image editing inconsistency**: Edits to specific regions often affect unintended areas
- **Background removal**: Real faces become generic; body parts may shift
- **Content restrictions**: Strict filtering on public figures, children in photos, and IP-sensitive material
- **Non-Latin text**: Weaker rendering of Chinese, Arabic, and Hebrew characters

## Use Cases

### Nano Banana 2 Excels At

- **High-resolution marketing assets**: 4K output for print, billboards, and large-format displays
- **Rapid prototyping**: Sub-6-second generation for iterative design workflows
- **Photorealistic portraits**: Human faces with natural lighting and textures
- **Cost-sensitive batch processing**: 50% cheaper than Nano Banana Pro at scale
- **Google ecosystem integration**: Native in Gemini app, Google Search AI Mode, and Lens

### GPT-4o Image Excels At

- **Text-heavy graphics**: Marketing mockups, social posts with typography
- **Conversational refinement**: Iterative editing through natural language
- **Complex multi-object scenes**: Maintaining consistency across 10-20 elements
- **Existing ChatGPT workflows**: Integrated with conversation context and memory

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Nano Banana 2 if:

- You need 4K resolution output
- Speed matters (4-6 seconds vs 10-20 seconds)
- You're optimizing for cost at scale
- Photorealistic human imagery is your primary use case
- You're already in the Google/Gemini ecosystem

### Choose GPT-4o Image if:

- Text accuracy is non-negotiable (marketing, signage, product mockups)
- You need conversational editing ("make the background warmer, keep the subject")
- Your workflow already lives in ChatGPT
- You're handling complex multi-object compositions
- You need the strongest prompt adherence for technical illustrations

### Consider Both if:

You're running a production pipeline that requires both high-resolution photorealistic outputs (Nano Banana 2) and precise text rendering (GPT-4o). Route tasks based on requirements rather than committing to a single provider.

## Bottom Line

Nano Banana 2 represents Google's most aggressive move in image generation: Pro-level quality at Flash speeds, 4K resolution, and pricing that undercuts both its predecessor and OpenAI. For most consumer and prosumer use cases—especially those requiring speed, resolution, or photorealism—Nano Banana 2 is the better choice.

GPT-4o Image retains its edge in text rendering, conversational workflows, and complex compositional control. If your primary use case involves typography or iterative editing through natural language, OpenAI's offering remains the benchmark.

The image generation market in 2026 no longer has a single winner. The right choice depends on your specific requirements—and increasingly, the optimal strategy is using both models for their respective strengths.
