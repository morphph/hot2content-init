## Quick Verdict
Claude Opus 4.6 leads in coding, agentic tasks, and complex reasoning with its massive 1M token context and Adaptive Thinking. Gemini 2.5 Pro excels in multimodal tasks (especially video and image understanding), offers a generous free tier, and benefits from deep Google ecosystem integration. Choose Opus for coding and agent workflows; choose Gemini for multimodal analysis and cost-sensitive applications.

## Best For
### Choose Gemini 2.5 Pro when:
- You need to process video or audio content natively
- Cost efficiency is a priority (2-4x cheaper than Opus)
- You're building within the Google Cloud ecosystem
- You need grounded search results integrated with generation
- Your application requires multimodal reasoning across different media types

### Choose Claude Opus 4.6 when:
- You need the best coding and agentic task performance
- You require 128K output tokens for long-form generation
- You want Adaptive Thinking for dynamic reasoning on complex problems
- You need Agent Teams for parallelized workflows
- You're building with the MCP ecosystem for tool integration

## Bottom Line
Gemini 2.5 Pro and Claude Opus 4.6 are both frontier models, but they have different strengths. Gemini leads in multimodal capabilities and cost efficiency, making it ideal for applications involving video, audio, and Google ecosystem integration. Claude Opus 4.6 dominates in coding, reasoning, and agentic capabilities, making it the go-to for software development and complex autonomous tasks. For many teams, the choice comes down to primary use case: multimodal applications favor Gemini, while coding and agent workflows favor Claude.
