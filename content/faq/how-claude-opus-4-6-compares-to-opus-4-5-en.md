# How Claude Opus 4.6 compares to Opus 4.5

## Key Differences Between Claude Opus 4.6 and Opus 4.5

Claude Opus 4.6 represents a significant upgrade over Opus 4.5 in three core areas: reasoning capabilities, context capacity, and multi-agent collaboration architecture.

The most striking improvements appear in specialized benchmarks. On Anthropic's internal Real-World Finance evaluation, Opus 4.6 improved by over 23 percentage points compared to Sonnet 4.5. It achieves state-of-the-art results on Finance Agent (60.7%) and TaxEval (76.0%). For enterprise applications, Opus 4.6 dominates GDPval-AA with an Elo rating of 1606, representing a +190 point improvement over Opus 4.5—indicating superior performance on economically valuable work.

The MRCR (Multi-document Reading Comprehension and Reasoning) performance improvements mean researchers can now use AI more effectively for literature reviews and complex document analysis.

One area where the difference is most visible to everyday users is UI generation. According to extensive Reddit testing, Opus 4.6 is "finally one-shotting complex UI" where Opus 4.5 required multiple iterations. Users report that 4.6 produces functional, polished interfaces on the first attempt, while 4.5 often needed refinement.

That said, Opus 4.5 remains a strong choice for certain use cases. It offers lower latency and a more predictable cost profile for straightforward tasks. Many users also prefer Opus 4.5's writing style, which is frequently cited as more natural and less mechanical than the heavily structured output of 4.6.

The choice between models depends on your priorities: Opus 4.6 excels at complex reasoning, financial analysis, and code generation, while Opus 4.5 may be preferable for general-purpose writing and tasks where response speed matters more than benchmark performance.

### What are the main benchmark improvements in Opus 4.6?

Opus 4.6 shows substantial gains across multiple evaluation categories. The Finance Agent benchmark improved to 60.7%, and TaxEval reached 76.0%—both state-of-the-art results as of early 2026. The GDPval-AA benchmark, which measures performance on economically valuable tasks, shows Opus 4.6 at 1606 Elo compared to Opus 4.5, a 190-point improvement.

The multi-document reasoning capabilities (MRCR) have improved enough that the model can now handle genuine academic literature reviews—a task where previous versions struggled with accuracy and coherence across multiple sources. These improvements stem from architectural upgrades to the reasoning engine and expanded context handling.

### Is Opus 4.5 still worth using?

Yes, Opus 4.5 remains highly capable and may be the better choice depending on your needs. It offers lower latency, which matters for real-time applications and interactive use cases. The cost profile is also more predictable for straightforward tasks.

Writing quality is subjective, but many users find Opus 4.5's output more natural and less formulaic. If you're primarily using Claude for content creation, drafting emails, or conversational interactions rather than complex analytical tasks, Opus 4.5's writing style advantage may outweigh 4.6's benchmark improvements. For budget-conscious teams running high-volume inference, the latency and cost characteristics of 4.5 remain attractive.

### How much better is Opus 4.6 at coding and UI generation?

The coding improvements are substantial, particularly for front-end work. Community testing on Reddit shows Opus 4.6 "one-shotting complex UI" consistently—producing complete, functional interfaces without the iterative refinement that Opus 4.5 typically required.

With Opus 4.5, users often needed to prompt multiple times to fix layout issues, missing components, or styling problems. Opus 4.6 generates more complete solutions on the first attempt. This represents both a productivity gain and a reduction in frustration for developers using Claude as a coding assistant. The improvement appears tied to enhanced spatial reasoning and better understanding of component hierarchies.

### What's different about multi-agent collaboration in Opus 4.6?

According to Anthropic, Opus 4.6 includes an upgraded multi-agent collaboration architecture. This affects how the model handles complex workflows where multiple AI agents coordinate on a task—common in agentic coding environments, research pipelines, and enterprise automation.

The practical impact is improved coherence when Claude operates within larger systems, better handoff between sub-tasks, and more reliable execution of multi-step plans. For developers building agentic applications or using Claude Code for complex refactoring, this architectural change translates to fewer failed tool calls and more consistent end-to-end task completion.

### Should I upgrade from Opus 4.5 to 4.6?

Consider upgrading if you work primarily with financial analysis, complex document reasoning, code generation, or UI development. The benchmark improvements in these areas are significant enough to justify the switch.

Stick with Opus 4.5 if latency is critical to your application, your budget requires predictable costs, or your use case centers on creative writing where you prefer the more natural output style. Many teams run both models, routing queries based on task type—using 4.6 for reasoning-heavy work and 4.5 for general-purpose interactions.
