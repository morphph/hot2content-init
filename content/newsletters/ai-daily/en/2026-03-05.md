## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: Mixture of Experts (MoE)

You're seeing "A3B" and "A17B" in model names like Qwen3.5-35B-A3B — that's Mixture of Experts. Instead of using all 35 billion parameters for every token, the model activates only 3 billion parameters at a time. Think of it like a hospital: you don't need every specialist for every patient. A router decides which "expert" sub-networks handle each input. The result? Near-full-size intelligence at a fraction of the compute cost. That's why the 35B-A3B model can run on a laptop — it only "thinks" with 3B parameters per token.

## 🎯 PICK OF THE DAY: GGML Joins Hugging Face

The merger of GGML/llama.cpp into Hugging Face is a watershed moment for local AI. Georgi Gerganov's GGML library is the engine that lets millions of people run AI models on their own hardware — laptops, phones, Raspberry Pis. By joining HF, the project gets sustainable funding and infrastructure while maintaining its open-source commitment. With Qwen 3.5 models already available in GGUF format (the GGML quantization format) pulling hundreds of thousands of downloads, local AI has never been more accessible. If you care about AI that doesn't depend on cloud APIs, this is the most important story of the week.
