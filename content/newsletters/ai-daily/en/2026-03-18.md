## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: What Is Context Engineering?

You'll see "context engineering" pop up in trending repos and agent discussions this week. It's a term that's replacing "prompt engineering" in the agent world — and for good reason.

Prompt engineering is about crafting the right text to send to a model. Context engineering is broader: it's about designing the entire information environment an agent operates in. That includes what documents it can retrieve, what tools it has access to, how previous conversation turns are summarized, and what gets pruned when the context window fills up.

Why it matters: a well-context-engineered agent with a mid-tier model will consistently outperform a frontier model with poor context management. The agent doesn't just need the right prompt — it needs the right information at the right time, and that requires deliberate system design, not just clever wording.
