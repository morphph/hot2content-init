## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: What Is a MoE (Mixture of Experts) Model?

A Mixture of Experts model splits its parameters into specialized "expert" sub-networks. For each input token, a router selects only a small subset of experts to activate — meaning a 397B parameter model might only use 17B parameters per token. This is why Simon Willison's friend can run Qwen 3.5 397B on a Mac with just 5.5GB of active memory: most of the model sits idle on disk for any given token. MoE gives you the knowledge of a massive model with the compute cost of a small one. The tradeoff? The full model still needs to be stored somewhere (209GB on disk in this case), and routing adds complexity.
