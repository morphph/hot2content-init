## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: Mixture of Experts (MoE)

Today's trending models highlight a pattern: Qwen3.5-35B-A3B has "A3B" in its name, and NVIDIA's Nemotron-3-Super-120B-A12B has "A12B." The "A" stands for **Active parameters**.

A Mixture of Experts (MoE) model has a total parameter count (e.g., 35B) but only activates a small subset (e.g., 3B) for any given input. A "router" network decides which expert subnetworks to activate based on the input. This is why Qwen 3.5 397B can run on an M3 Mac with only 5.5 GB of active memory — while the full model is 209GB on disk, only a tiny fraction is active at any moment. MoE gives you big-model quality at small-model inference cost.

## 🎯 PICK OF THE DAY: Claude Cowork Dispatch

The most interesting launch today isn't a new model — it's a UX paradigm shift. Dispatch turns Claude Cowork into a persistent, always-running agent on your computer that you can message from your phone. Think of it as an async coding partner: you send a message while commuting, and by the time you're at your desk, the work is done.

What makes it significant:
- **Persistent context** — One continuous conversation that maintains state across sessions
- **Mobile-first interaction** — Break the "must be at my laptop" constraint for AI coding
- **Claude Code integration** — Can now spin up full Claude Code sessions, not just chat
- **Practical autonomy** — As Emollick noted, it covers most of what people were building complex remote setups for

This signals where AI coding tools are heading: away from "chat with a model" toward "delegate to a teammate." The fact that it launched alongside Cursor's Composer 2 and Google's vibe coding tools makes today feel like a turning point in how developers interact with AI assistants.
