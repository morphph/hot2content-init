## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: Mixture of Experts (MoE)

Many of this week's trending models — Qwen 3.5-35B-A3B, Qwen 3.5-397B-A17B, GLM-5 — use **Mixture of Experts (MoE)** architecture. The "A" number tells you how many parameters are *active* per token.

In a traditional "dense" model, every parameter processes every token. In an MoE model, a **router** selects only a subset of specialized "expert" sub-networks for each token. So Qwen 3.5-35B-A3B has 35 billion total parameters but only activates 3 billion per token — giving you near-35B quality at near-3B inference cost.

**Why it matters:** MoE lets you scale model knowledge without scaling inference cost linearly. It's why you can run a "35B" model on hardware that could barely handle a 7B dense model.

HuggingFace just published a comprehensive guide on MoE in Transformers this week — worth a read.

[Read more →](https://huggingface.co/blog/moe-transformers)

X's Grok crisis is worth watching closely. Unlike previous AI safety incidents that happened in labs or controlled environments, Grok's offensive outputs are being generated *and posted publicly on social media* — essentially weaponizing an AI chatbot as a content generator on the platform that hosts it.
