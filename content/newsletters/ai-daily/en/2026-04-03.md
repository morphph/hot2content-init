## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: What's a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) Model?

Gemma 4's 26B-A4B uses MoE architecture — but what does that mean? A Mixture-of-Experts model has many "expert" sub-networks but only activates a few for each input token. The "26B-A4B" means 26 billion total parameters, but only ~4 billion are active per token. This gives you the quality of a large model with the speed and cost of a small one. It's why MoE models can run on laptops while competing with dense models many times their size. Think of it as having a team of specialists where only the relevant ones work on each task, rather than making everyone do everything.

## 🎯 PICK OF THE DAY: Anthropic's Emotion Concepts Research

Today's standout is Anthropic's new interpretability paper on emotion in LLMs. This isn't about whether AI "feels" — it's mechanistic: the researchers found actual internal representations corresponding to emotion concepts that can causally influence Claude's outputs. Sometimes in ways the model's designers didn't intend. This matters because it bridges the gap between "Claude acts emotional sometimes" (observable) and "here's the computational mechanism behind it" (explainable). As models get more capable and autonomous, understanding these internal dynamics becomes critical for alignment. It's one of the clearest examples yet of interpretability research producing actionable safety insights.
[Read more →](https://www.anthropic.com/research/emotion-concepts-function)
