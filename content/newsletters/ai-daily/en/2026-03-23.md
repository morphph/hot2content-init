## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)

This week saw multiple MoE releases: Mistral Small 4 (119B/6B active), Nemotron-Cascade 2 (30B/3B active), Qwen 3.5-35B-A3B.

**What is MoE?** Instead of running all parameters on every input, a MoE model has many "expert" sub-networks and a router that selects only a few for each token. A 119B model might only activate 6B parameters per forward pass — getting big-model quality at small-model speed and cost.

**Why it matters:** MoE is becoming the dominant architecture for efficient frontier models. You get the knowledge capacity of a huge model with the inference cost of a small one. The tradeoff: more total parameters means more VRAM for weights, even if compute per token is low.

The biggest story this week isn't a launch — it's a disclosure. Cursor built Composer 2, its flagship coding model, on top of Moonshot AI's open-source Kimi K2.5 from China. Users traced internal identifiers and forced the admission.

Why this matters beyond the drama:

- **Attribution matters.** The initial lack of disclosure triggered a trust crisis in the developer community.
- **The competitive landscape is shifting.** If Kimi 2.5 can be fine-tuned to match Opus 4.6, Kimi 3.0 could surpass it — and it'll be open-source.

The age of "which API do you call" is giving way to "which open model do you fine-tune." That's a fundamental power shift.
