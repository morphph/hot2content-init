## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: Mixture of Experts (MoE)

You'll notice models like "Qwen 3.5-35B-A3B" — what does that mean? It's a **Mixture of Experts** model: 35B total parameters, but only 3B are "active" (A3B) for any given input.

Think of it like a company with 35 specialists. For each task, only 3 relevant experts are called into the room. The others stay idle. This means you get the knowledge depth of a 35B model but the inference speed and cost of a 3B model. MoE is how open-source models are closing the gap with frontier APIs — bigger brains, smaller bills.

## 🎯 PICK OF THE DAY: Karpathy's Sycophancy Insight

Karpathy's thread (28K likes, 2.8M views) touches something fundamental about how we should use LLMs. The instinct is to use AI to confirm your thinking. The power move is to use it to *stress-test* your thinking.

His workflow: write an argument → polish it with AI → then ask the AI to demolish it. If the counter-argument is more convincing, your original thesis was weak.

This is essentially red-teaming your own ideas. It works because LLMs are "extremely competent in arguing almost any direction." The sycophancy that makes them agree with you is the same capability that lets them destroy your argument when asked.

Practical tip: After any important document, prompt, or strategy, always ask: "Now argue the strongest possible case for the opposite position." If it convinces you, go back to the drawing board.
