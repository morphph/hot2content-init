## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: What Are Subagents?

Subagents are specialized AI agents spawned by a "parent" agent to handle specific subtasks. Think of them as delegated workers: when your main coding agent encounters a complex project, instead of trying to do everything in one context window, it can spin up subagents — one to handle the frontend, one for the backend, one for tests.

The key advantages: (1) **Context isolation** — each subagent gets a clean context window focused on its task, avoiding the quality degradation that comes from overloaded contexts. (2) **Parallelism** — multiple subagents can work simultaneously on different parts of a project. (3) **Steerability** — you can redirect individual subagents without disrupting the whole workflow.

OpenAI's Codex subagents and Claude Code's multi-agent capabilities both implement this pattern, and it's becoming the standard architecture for complex agentic coding tasks.

## 🎯 PICK OF THE DAY: Alibaba's SWE-CI Benchmark

The most consequential finding this week isn't a new model — it's a new way to evaluate them. Alibaba's SWE-CI benchmark tested 18 AI coding agents not on whether they could write code, but whether code they maintained over 233 days still worked.

The results are sobering: 75% of models introduced regressions during maintenance. Technical debt compounded until codebases collapsed. Only Claude Opus 4.5/4.6 maintained a >50% zero-regression rate.

This matters because the industry has been optimizing for "snapshot" benchmarks — can the model solve this problem right now? SWE-CI asks the harder question: can it be a reliable long-term collaborator? The answer, for most models, is not yet. This benchmark will likely reshape how we evaluate and choose coding agents for real-world use.
