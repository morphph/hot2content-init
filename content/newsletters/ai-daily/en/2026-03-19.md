## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: What Are Subagents?

Subagents are specialized AI agents spawned by a "parent" agent to handle specific subtasks. Think of them like delegating work to team members:

- **Why they matter**: A single agent's context window fills up fast. Subagents let the main agent stay focused while delegating research, code review, or testing to separate contexts.
- **How they work**: The parent agent decides when to spawn a subagent, gives it a specific task, and the subagent works independently — sometimes in parallel with other subagents.
- **Real-world example**: In Codex, you might have one subagent refactoring the backend while another writes tests, while your main agent coordinates the overall plan.
- **Key limitation**: Subagents can't directly communicate with each other — they report back to the parent, which orchestrates the workflow.

The pattern is catching on fast: Codex, Claude Code, and LangGraph all now support some form of multi-agent orchestration.

## 🎯 PICK OF THE DAY: Claude Cowork Dispatch

Today's standout is Dispatch — Anthropic's answer to "I want my AI to keep working when I'm away from my computer."

**What it is**: A persistent Claude conversation that runs on your desktop but can be messaged from your phone. Start a complex task, walk away, check progress from your phone, and come back to completed work.

**Why it matters**: This bridges the gap between synchronous AI chat and asynchronous AI agents. You don't need a server, a cloud deployment, or a CLI setup — just Claude Desktop and your phone.

**The reaction**: Ethan Mollick noted it covers "90% of what I was trying to use OpenClaw for" with less risk. The feature went from research preview to 100% Pro rollout in a single day, suggesting strong internal confidence.

**The bigger picture**: The trend is clear — AI assistants are evolving from "chat when you're at your desk" to "always-on collaborators." Between Claude Dispatch, Codex subagents, and Claude Code scheduled tasks, the agentic future is arriving faster than expected.
