## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: Mixture of Experts (MoE)

Google's new Gemma 4 26B-A4B is an MoE model — but what does that mean? A Mixture of Experts model has many "expert" sub-networks inside it, but only activates a small subset for each input token. The "26B-A4B" naming tells you: 26 billion total parameters, but only 4 billion are **A**ctive per token. This means you get the knowledge capacity of a much larger model with the speed and memory footprint of a smaller one. It's why MoE models can run on consumer hardware while punching above their weight. Think of it like a company with 100 specialists — for any given question, you only need 3-4 of them in the meeting.

## 🎯 PICK OF THE DAY: Karpathy's LLM Knowledge Base Workflow

Andrej Karpathy's thread on building personal knowledge bases with LLMs is the most thought-provoking post of the day (30K+ likes). The core idea: stop using LLMs just for code — use them to compile, maintain, and query structured knowledge wikis. His stack is simple (markdown + Obsidian + CLI tools) but the implications are profound. At ~400K words, no RAG needed. The LLM auto-maintains indices and summaries. Outputs go back into the wiki, so your explorations always "add up." He even runs LLM "health checks" to find inconsistencies and impute missing data. If you're doing any kind of research, this workflow is worth stealing immediately.
