## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: What Is a "Knowledge Graph" and Why Do Agents Need One?

When you ask an AI agent to work on a large codebase, it reads files one at a time — like trying to understand a city by reading individual street signs. A knowledge graph is a structured map of relationships: this function calls that function, this module depends on that module, this concept relates to that concept. Instead of searching through raw text, an agent with a knowledge graph can reason about *connections* — "if I change this API, what breaks downstream?" Several new tools (like graphify) are building knowledge graphs automatically from code and documents, giving agents something closer to the structural understanding that experienced developers carry in their heads.
