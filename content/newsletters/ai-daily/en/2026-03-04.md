## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: Dense vs. MoE — When to Choose What

Yesterday we covered Mixture of Experts (MoE) models. Today's complement: dense models.

A **dense model** activates all its parameters for every token. Qwen3.5-27B uses all 27 billion parameters every time. An **MoE model** like Qwen3.5-35B-A3B has 35B total but only uses 3B per token.

**When to use dense:** Consistent latency requirements, memory-constrained deployments (paradoxically — a 9B dense model uses less total RAM than a 35B MoE), simpler serving infrastructure, and when you need predictable per-token costs.

**When to use MoE:** Maximum capability per compute dollar, high-throughput batch processing, and when you have enough RAM to load the full model but want fast inference.

Qwen3.5 offering both architectures across the size spectrum is a gift to practitioners — you can benchmark both approaches for your specific use case and pick the one that fits.

[Read more →](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3.5-27B)
