## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY: Mixture of Experts (MoE)

**What it is:** Instead of running all parameters for every token, a MoE model routes each token to a small subset of specialized "expert" sub-networks. Qwen3.5-397B-A17B, for example, has 397 billion total parameters but only activates 17 billion per token.

**Why it matters:** You get frontier-level quality at a fraction of the compute cost. It's why Qwen3.5 models can match Claude Sonnet 4.5 while running on consumer hardware. The tradeoff: MoE models need more VRAM to load (all experts must be in memory) but use less compute per inference. HuggingFace just published an updated deep-dive on MoE in Transformers this week.

[Read more →](https://huggingface.co/blog/moe-transformers)
