## 🏆 Cover Story: OpenAI Ships Two Models in Two Days — GPT-5.4 Brings Native Computer Use

OpenAI went on a shipping spree this week. GPT-5.3 Instant dropped on March 3, followed just two days later by GPT-5.4 Thinking and GPT-5.4 Pro on March 5.

**GPT-5.3 Instant** (Mar 3) is a tone update — OpenAI says it "reduces the cringe," making everyday ChatGPT conversations more direct and natural. Available to all users; API identifier `gpt-5.3-chat-latest`. Legacy GPT-5.2 Instant remains accessible until June 3. ([Source](https://9to5mac.com/2026/03/03/openai-releases-gpt-5-3-instant-update-to-make-chatgpt-less-cringe/))

**GPT-5.4 Thinking / Pro** (Mar 5) is the real headline:
- **Native Computer Use**: The model can directly interact with desktop interfaces; agentic task completion jumped significantly from GPT-5.2's 47.3%
- **1M token context window**
- **Finance plugins**: ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets (beta), plus integrations with FactSet, MSCI, Moody's, and Third Bridge
- **Visible reasoning**: In ChatGPT, the model shows its thinking plan upfront; users can redirect mid-reasoning
- First general-purpose model with built-in cybersecurity high-capability mitigations

OpenAI positions this as "our most capable and efficient frontier model for professional work" — a clear enterprise play. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/05/openai-launches-gpt-5-4-with-pro-and-thinking-versions/)) ([VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/openai-launches-gpt-5-4-with-native-computer-use-mode-financial-plugins-for))

## 🔥 Top Stories

### 1. Pentagon Labels Anthropic a "Supply Chain Risk" — Silicon Valley Rattled

The most consequential story this week: the Pentagon officially notified Anthropic of its "supply chain risk" designation. Under this label, **no contractor, supplier, or partner doing business with the U.S. military may conduct commercial activity with Anthropic**.

The move originated from a Trump administration decision in late February. Anthropic responded forcefully, vowing to "challenge any supply chain risk designation in court" and calling it "a dangerous precedent for any American company that negotiates with the government."

**Microsoft stepped in**: its legal team concluded that Anthropic products, including Claude, can remain available to customers through M365, GitHub, and Microsoft AI Foundry — military contracts excluded.

The implications extend far beyond Anthropic. Every AI company is now reassessing its government relations playbook. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/05/tech/pentagon-anthropic-supply-chain-risk)) ([WIRED](https://www.wired.com/story/anthropic-supply-chain-risk-shockwaves-silicon-valley/)) ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/05/microsoft-says-anthropics-products-can-remain-available-to-customers-after-security-risk-designation.html))

### 2. Jensen Huang: NVIDIA's Investments in OpenAI and Anthropic Are "Likely the Last"

At the Morgan Stanley TMT Conference, NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang revealed that the company's $30B investment in OpenAI and $10B in Anthropic will likely be its final stakes in both companies — as they prepare for 2026 IPOs.

"I think the opportunity to invest $100 billion in OpenAI is probably not in the cards," Huang said. The actual OpenAI investment came in well below earlier commitments, and the reasons remain murky. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/nvidia-will-not-be-able-invest-100-billion-openai-due-ipo-ceo-jensen-says-2026-03-04/)) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/04/jensen-huang-says-nvidia-is-pulling-back-from-openai-and-anthropic-but-his-explanation-raises-more-questions-than-it-answers/))

### 3. Big Tech CEOs Sign "Ratepayer Protection Pledge" at the White House

CEOs from Google, Microsoft, Meta, Oracle, xAI, OpenAI, and Amazon signed the **Ratepayer Protection Pledge** — committing to cover all energy costs for their data centers rather than passing them to consumers.

The backdrop: surging AI data center power demand has become a political liability ahead of the 2026 midterms. Trump first announced the pledge during his State of the Union address. ([The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/mar/04/us-tech-companies-energy-cost-pledge-white-house)) ([White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2026/03/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-advances-energy-affordability-with-the-ratepayer-protection-pledge/))

## 🏢 Platform Moves

**Apple Launches $599 MacBook Neo**
Apple unveiled the MacBook Neo at its March event — a $599 entry-level MacBook powered by an iPhone-class A-series chip, with a 13-inch display and 8GB RAM. It's Apple's biggest push into the budget laptop market, directly targeting Chromebooks and low-end Windows PCs. Not an AI product per se, but its Apple Intelligence capabilities make it relevant. ([The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/tech/886496/apple-march-2026-event-macbook-neo-announcement)) ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/apple-launches-599-macbook-neo-threatening-windows-pc-market))

**Apple × Google: Gemini-Powered Siri Accelerates**
Apple is reportedly considering having Google run Gemini-powered Siri features on Apple-controlled servers, rather than relying entirely on external cloud infrastructure. The partnership announced in January is moving fast. ([India Today](https://www.indiatoday.in/technology/news/story/apple-to-supercharge-siri-with-gemini-google-servers-may-help-speed-up-apple-intelligence-2876820-2026-03-03))

**Meta Tests AI Shopping Tool**
Meta is testing a shopping research feature in its AI chatbot, competing directly with ChatGPT and Gemini's commerce capabilities. Notably, researchers found Meta AI routes some queries through Google's Gemini 3 internally — the final product may be a multi-model system. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/meta-tests-ai-shopping-research-tool-to-rival-chatgpt-gemini)) ([CNET](https://www.cnet.com/tech/services-and-software/meta-new-ai-shopping-tool/))

**Broadcom: AI Chip Revenue to Exceed $100B by 2027**
Broadcom CEO Hock Tan projected AI chip revenue will "significantly exceed $100 billion in 2027" on the company's earnings call. Key clients include Google (TPUs), OpenAI, and Meta. Broadcom is also delivering 1GW of TPU compute for Anthropic in 2026. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/04/broadcom-sees-ai-chip-sales-significantly-over-100-billion-in-2027.html))

2. **Anthropic's political crisis is an industry-wide warning.** The "supply chain risk" label carries systemic consequences. Every AI company is now recalibrating its government relations strategy.

3. **The AI IPO wave is imminent.** NVIDIA pulling back, companies preparing to list — OpenAI and Anthropic IPOs in 2026 look increasingly certain. This will be a watershed moment for the industry.

4. **The compute arms race isn't slowing down.** Broadcom's $100B projection, NVIDIA's multi-billion investments, the White House energy pledge — infrastructure spending continues on an exponential trajectory.

5. **Apple's AI strategy is crystallizing.** $599 MacBook Neo + Siri × Gemini: Apple is using hardware to pave the way and Google's models to fill capability gaps. The affordable device + strong AI combination is worth watching.
