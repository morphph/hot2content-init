## 🏆 Cover Story: Anthropic Sues the Pentagon — AI Ethics Gets Real

The biggest story this week isn't a model launch. It's a standoff.

After refusing to let Claude be used for mass domestic surveillance and autonomous lethal weapons, Anthropic was designated a "supply chain risk" by the Pentagon. On March 9, the company filed two lawsuits against the Trump administration to vacate the designation — a move that could cost "hundreds of millions of dollars" in revenue.

The timing was extraordinary. On the same day Anthropic filed suit:
- It launched **Claude Code Review**, a multi-agent system for automated code analysis
- **Microsoft announced Copilot Cowork**, built on Anthropic's Claude Cowork technology, as part of M365 Copilot Wave 3
- Microsoft's legal team confirmed Claude remains available to customers outside the Department of Defense

Trump responded by ordering all government agencies to stop using Claude, posting that he "fired Anthropic like dogs."

The core question: **Can an AI company draw ethical red lines and survive?** Anthropic is betting yes.
