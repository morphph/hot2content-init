## 🏆 Cover Story: NVIDIA Invests $30B in OpenAI — The Chip Giant Becomes a Shareholder

NVIDIA is close to finalizing a **$30 billion equity investment in OpenAI**, part of a fundraising round where OpenAI is seeking over **$100 billion** at a **$730 billion pre-money valuation**. This replaces a previous, more complex $100B infrastructure partnership announced last September.

SoftBank, Amazon, and Microsoft are also expected participants. OpenAI plans to deploy **$600 billion on compute infrastructure**.

The strategic implications are enormous: NVIDIA is transitioning from being merely OpenAI's most critical supplier to becoming a direct stakeholder — deepening an already unprecedented vendor-customer relationship.

## 🔥 Top Stories

### 1. Anthropic Launches Claude Sonnet 4.6 — Second Major Model in 12 Days

Following Claude Opus 4.6 on Feb 5, Anthropic released **Claude Sonnet 4.6** on Feb 17 — delivering near-Opus-level performance at Sonnet pricing (**$3/$15 per million tokens**).

Key highlights:
- Significant improvements in coding, computer use, design, and knowledge work
- 1M token context window (beta)
- Now the **default model for Free and Pro users** on Claude chatbot and Claude Cowork
- Free tier upgraded with file creation, connectors, skills, and compaction

The message is clear: frontier-class capabilities are rapidly becoming commodity-priced.

### 3. India AI Impact Summit: $250B+ in Global Commitments

The **India AI Impact Summit 2026** in New Delhi drew the biggest names in AI and produced staggering investment pledges:

- **Reliance**: $110B for data centers and infrastructure
- **Adani Group**: $100B to expand AI data centers (2GW → 5GW)
- **Microsoft**: On track for $50B by 2030 for AI in the Global South
- **Google**: $15B for India AI infrastructure + $30M AI for Science Challenge
- **Attendees**: Sam Altman, Dario Amodei, Sundar Pichai, Demis Hassabis, and more

India also launched the **MANAV Framework** for human-centric AI and signed the US-led **Pax Silica** agreement on silicon supply chain security.

### 4. UK Mandates 48-Hour Takedown for Non-Consensual Intimate Images

The UK is amending its **Crime and Policing Bill** to require tech platforms to remove non-consensual intimate images — including **AI-generated deepfakes** — within **48 hours** of a report. Penalties: up to **10% of global revenue** or service blocking.

The move was catalyzed by a recent flood of AI-generated nude images on X.

📎 [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/legal/litigation/uk-impose-48-hour-online-takedown-rule-nonconsensual-intimate-images-2026-02-18/) · [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-18/uk-to-force-tech-firms-to-take-down-abusive-images-in-48-hours)
