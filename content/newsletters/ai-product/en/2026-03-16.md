## 🏆 Cover Story: Anthropic vs. the Pentagon — Silicon Valley Picks a Side

The biggest AI story this week isn't a product launch. It's a lawsuit.

On March 9, Anthropic sued the U.S. Department of Defense after Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth designated the company a "supply chain risk" — a label historically reserved for foreign adversaries like Huawei. The reason: Anthropic refused to lift Claude's restrictions on autonomous weapons and domestic surveillance.

The response from the industry was extraordinary. **Over 30 OpenAI and Google DeepMind employees signed a statement supporting Anthropic.** Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Apple filed court briefs in Anthropic's defense. The message from Silicon Valley is clear: the government cannot punish a company for maintaining AI safety guardrails.

Meanwhile, on the same day, OpenAI secured a deal allowing its technology to be used in classified military systems.

## 🔥 Top Stories

### Microsoft 365 Copilot Wave 3 + Copilot Cowork
Microsoft launched the third major wave of M365 Copilot, headlined by **Copilot Cowork** — a long-running, multi-step AI agent built on Anthropic's Claude Cowork technology. It runs in a sandboxed cloud environment, handles complex tasks across M365 apps, and keeps working as you switch devices. Built on a $30B Azure compute deal with Anthropic, this signals Microsoft's definitive shift to a multi-model strategy beyond its OpenAI partnership.

👉 [Microsoft Blog](https://blogs.microsoft.com/blog/2026/03/09/introducing-the-first-frontier-suite-built-on-intelligence-trust/) · [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/09/microsoft-copilot-cowork-ai-agents-anthropic-e7-m365-saas/) · [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/orchestration/microsoft-announces-copilot-cowork-with-help-from-anthropic-a-cloud-powered)

### Meta's "Avocado" Model Delayed
Meta's next-gen flagship AI model (codename: Avocado), originally slated for March, has been pushed to May–June. NYT reports the model is trailing Google Gemini on benchmarks — a setback for Meta's AI ambitions.

👉 [Financial Express](https://www.financialexpress.com/life/technology-metas-avocado-ai-launch-delayed-alexandr-wangs-msl-extends-launch-to-mid-2026-as-flagship-model-trails-google-gemini-4171447/) · [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/13/ai-super-team-mark-zuckerberg-google-gemini/)

### NVIDIA GTC 2026 Kicks Off Today
GTC 2026 opens today in San Jose (March 16–19). Jensen Huang's keynote is expected to formally unveil **NemoClaw**, an open-source enterprise AI agent platform for safe agent deployment. NVIDIA is also expected to showcase a new inference chip system developed with Groq.

The bigger bombshell: **NVIDIA is committing $26 billion to train its own open-weight AI models**, positioning itself to compete directly with OpenAI, Anthropic, and DeepSeek. Forbes analysis suggests the play is to ensure 90% of AI research stays on CUDA.

## 🤖 Model & API Updates

### OpenAI Retires GPT-5.1 Series
As of March 11, GPT-5.1 Instant, GPT-5.1 Thinking, and GPT-5.1 Pro are no longer available in ChatGPT. Existing conversations auto-migrate to GPT-5.3/5.4 counterparts. The deprecation cycle keeps accelerating.

## 🏢 Platform Moves

### Google: Gemini Goes Deep into Workspace + Maps Overhaul
Google shipped on two fronts this week:
- **Workspace**: Gemini can now pull from your Docs, Sheets, Drive, emails, and Chat history to generate fully formatted content from a single prompt. Available for AI Ultra subscribers.
- **Maps**: Launched "Ask Maps," a Gemini-powered conversational feature, plus a complete 3D immersive navigation redesign — the biggest Maps navigation update in a decade.

👉 [Google Blog](https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/workspace/gemini-workspace-updates-march-2026/) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/12/google-maps-is-getting-an-ai-ask-maps-feature-and-upgraded-immersive-navigation/) · [Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2026/03/google-maps-gets-its-biggest-navigation-redesign-in-a-decade-plus-more-ai/)

### U.S. Senate Approves AI Chatbots for Official Use
The Senate Sergeant at Arms' CIO authorized Senate staff to use **ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot** for official work — editing documents, summarizing information, and preparing briefing materials. The House had previously approved these plus Claude. Notably, Claude was not included in the Senate authorization.

## 🎯 Takeaways

1. **AI safety is now a legal battleground.** Anthropic's lawsuit proves that "responsible AI" can mean going to court against your biggest potential customer. The industry rallied behind them — for now.
2. **Microsoft's multi-model strategy is real.** Copilot Cowork runs on Anthropic, not OpenAI. The $30B Azure deal makes the dual-partnership viable.
3. **NVIDIA is becoming a full-stack AI player.** $26B for open-weight models + NemoClaw agent platform = Jensen is playing a much bigger game than selling GPUs.
4. **Google's Gemini infiltration continues.** From Workspace to Maps, Google is embedding Gemini into every user touchpoint it owns.
5. **Government AI adoption is accelerating — with political complications.** The Senate greenlighted ChatGPT/Gemini/Copilot but excluded Claude. Hard to separate that from the Pentagon saga.
