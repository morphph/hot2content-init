## 🏆 Cover Story: Anthropic's "Claude Mythos" Leak Shakes the Industry — IPO Imminent

The biggest AI story of the week came from a door left unlocked. A misconfigured CMS at Anthropic left nearly 3,000 internal documents — draft blog posts, PDFs, internal memos — sitting in a publicly searchable, unencrypted database. Among them: materials for a model called **Claude Mythos** (internal codename "Capybara"), described as "by far the most powerful AI model we've ever developed."

After Fortune broke the story, Anthropic didn't deny it — they confirmed the model exists, calling it "a step change" in capabilities.

What the leaked drafts reveal:
- Mythos is **a new tier above Opus**, not a version update
- It scores "dramatically higher" than Opus 4.6 on coding, reasoning, and cybersecurity
- It is "currently far ahead of any other AI model in cyber capabilities"
- Anthropic acknowledges it is "very expensive for us to serve"

The irony writes itself: the AI safety company's biggest secret was exposed because of a default CMS setting nobody changed.

**Markets reacted swiftly.** Cybersecurity stocks tanked Friday — CrowdStrike fell 7%, Palo Alto Networks dropped 6%. The implication: if next-gen AI can "exploit vulnerabilities in ways that far outpace the efforts of defenders," the cybersecurity playbook needs rewriting.

Meanwhile, Bloomberg reported that Anthropic is in **early IPO talks** with Wall Street banks, targeting **as soon as October 2026**. The offering could raise $60B+, making it the second-largest IPO in history behind SpaceX. The company's latest Series G valued it at $380 billion.

On the other side, OpenAI's next-gen model codenamed **"Spud"** reportedly finished pretraining on March 25. Sam Altman told staff: "Things are moving faster than many of us expected."

The frontier model race is accelerating. So is the compute bill.

## 🔥 Top Stories This Week

### 1. OpenAI Kills Sora, Enters "Pre-IPO Focus Mode"

OpenAI officially shut down its Sora video generation app and API on March 24 — just six months after launch. The reasons: unsustainable GPU costs, declining engagement, and deepfake abuse. According to WIRED, this is OpenAI's strategic narrowing before an IPO — cutting consumer creative tools to concentrate on the ChatGPT superapp and enterprise coding platforms.

ChatGPT Pro has reportedly jumped to $200/month (from $20), offering a dedicated GPU slice and 1M-token context window.

### 2. ChatGPT Shopping Pivot: From Checkout to Discovery

OpenAI overhauled ChatGPT's shopping experience on March 24, abandoning the failed Instant Checkout feature in favor of **product discovery** powered by the **Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP)**. The new experience supports visual browsing, image search, and side-by-side comparisons, with partners including Walmart, Target, Best Buy, Sephora, and Nordstrom.

In a parallel move, Shopify launched **Agentic Storefronts** and co-developed the **Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)** with Google, enabling merchants to sell directly inside ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and other AI assistants.

The **AI commerce standard war** has quietly begun.

### 3. Apple Opens Siri to All AI Models

Bloomberg reports that iOS 27 will allow Gemini, Claude, and other rival AIs to plug into the iPhone via a new Extensions system. Apple is also building its own chatbot internally (codenamed **"Campos"**), reportedly powered by Google's Gemini. WWDC dates are confirmed, with AI and Siri as centerpieces.

Apple's strategy is crystallizing: **don't build the best model — build the best dispatch platform.** The iPhone could become AI's largest distribution channel.

The new ARC-AGI-3 benchmark scored **every frontier model below 1%** on tasks that 100% of humans solved on their first try. Gemini 3.1 Pro led at 0.37%. Grok 4.2 scored a clean 0%.

A sobering reminder: the gap between pattern-matching and genuine abstract reasoning remains vast.

### 5. Wikipedia Officially Bans AI-Generated Content

English Wikipedia formally adopted a policy on March 20 that **prohibits using LLMs to generate or rewrite article content**. Limited exceptions remain for editing assistance and translation. The previous language ("should not be used") has been replaced with an outright ban.

In an age of AI content saturation, humanity's largest knowledge repository drew a hard line.

## 🏢 Platform Moves

### Anthropic: From Leak to IPO in One Week

Beyond the Mythos leak, Anthropic also dealt with a 6+ hour Claude outage on March 28 (affecting web, API, and Claude Code), underscoring infrastructure strain from rapid user growth. A Claude Desktop bug where Dispatch sessions stopped responding was fixed in v1.1.9493.

The IPO signal means Anthropic must now demonstrate a sustainable business model in public markets. The leaked detail that Mythos is "very expensive to serve" raises key questions about pricing strategy and customer acquisition.

### OpenAI: Strategic Contraction, Three-Pillar Focus

Kill Sora, upgrade shopping, supercharge Codex plugins — OpenAI's moves this week outline a clear pre-IPO playbook: ① ChatGPT as the consumer super-app; ② Codex as the enterprise developer platform; ③ Advertising as the new revenue engine. SoftBank continues backing with a $40B loan commitment.

Reuters also reported OpenAI is exploring a PE joint venture structure to subsidize enterprise customization costs — a direct counter to Anthropic's rapid enterprise gains.

### Google: The Most Prolific Week

Google shipped more this week than anyone: Gemini 3.1 Flash Live (real-time multimodal), Search Live (global rollout), Lyria 3 Pro (music), chat history import, TurboQuant (6x KV Cache compression). The strategy: **make Gemini so sticky you never leave.**

The chat import tool deserves special attention — allowing users to migrate conversation history from ChatGPT and Claude is a direct challenge to platform lock-in.

### Meta: Open Source + Commerce in Parallel

Released SAM 3.1 (image segmentation) and TRIBE v2 (brain encoding) as major open-source contributions. On the commercial side, unveiled AI shopping features for Instagram and Facebook at Shoptalk 2026: AI product recommendations, review summaries, and creator affiliate marketing. Meta also faces a US class action over Ray-Ban AI glasses privacy violations.

### Huawei 950PR: A Potential Inflection Point for China

Alibaba and ByteDance are reportedly ordering Huawei's 950PR AI chip after improved CUDA compatibility tests. Huawei targets ~750,000 shipments in 2026. If successful, this would be the most significant shift in China's AI infrastructure since US export controls began.

## 🎯 Takeaway

**The keyword this week: Fracture.**

The AI industry experienced dramatic splits across every dimension in a single week:

1. **Two IPO races.** Anthropic and OpenAI are both sprinting toward public listings, which means every product decision now serves the narrative of "how do we tell our story to public markets." Sora wasn't killed for failing technically — it was killed for not fitting the IPO thesis.

2. **AI commerce standard war begins.** OpenAI's ACP vs. Shopify/Google's UCP — whoever defines how AI agents shop on behalf of users controls the gateway to the next trillion-dollar market.

3. **Apple's platform bet.** Turning Siri into a multi-model dispatch layer means Apple is betting not on which model wins, but that **the interface matters more than the engine.** If they're right, the iPhone becomes AI's App Store.

4. **Safety ≠ Secure.** Anthropic's leak and Mythos's cybersecurity warnings create a sharp paradox: the most prominent AI safety company simultaneously caused a security incident (the leak) and created a security threat (a model that outpaces cyber defenders).

5. **ARC-AGI-3's reminder.** While everyone shouts "AGI," a simple abstract reasoning test handed every frontier model a near-zero score. Hype is easy to manufacture. Benchmarks don't lie.
