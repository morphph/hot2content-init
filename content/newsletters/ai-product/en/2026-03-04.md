## 🏆 Cover Story: AI Meets the Pentagon — The Week That Changed Everything

This may be the most consequential week in AI industry history.

Anthropic refused to let Claude be used for autonomous weapons or mass surveillance. The Pentagon labeled it a "supply-chain risk to national security." Hours later, Trump ordered **all federal agencies to immediately cease using Anthropic products**. That same day, OpenAI announced a deal to deploy AI on the military's classified networks.

Then it got surreal: the Wall Street Journal reported that the U.S. military **still used Claude** during strikes on Iran — to help with target identification — hours after the ban was announced.

On Monday, Sam Altman admitted the deal was "rushed" and "looked opportunistic and sloppy," announcing contract amendments: an explicit ban on domestic surveillance, and a prohibition on use by intelligence agencies (NSA, DIA, NGA) without separate authorization.

**Why it matters:** AI companies are no longer just vendors — they're geopolitical actors. The tension between safety principles, commercial incentives, and government pressure will define this industry for years.

## 🎯 Takeaways

1. **AI safety is now a geopolitical issue.** When government contracts and military strikes are on the table, every AI company must pick a side. Anthropic chose principles and paid the price. OpenAI chose pragmatism and faces a trust crisis.

2. **"AI layoffs" have entered the mainstream narrative.** Block's case will become a template. Expect more companies to use AI as restructuring rationale — whether it's genuine efficiency or convenient cover.

3. **The infrastructure arms race is intensifying.** Meta-AMD's $100B deal, Google's aggressive efficiency-tier pricing, Apple going all-in on Google Cloud. The 2026 AI war isn't just about models — it's about compute and ecosystems.

4. **Efficiency models are the real growth engine.** Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite's pricing signals that "good enough and cheap enough" wins at scale. The frontier model race grabs headlines; the efficiency race captures markets.
