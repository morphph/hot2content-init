## 🏆 Cover Story: NVIDIA GTC 2026 — Jensen's Trillion-Dollar Bet

GTC 2026 kicked off in San Jose this week, and Jensen Huang delivered arguably the most ambitious product roadmap in NVIDIA's history:

- **Vera Rubin platform unveiled**: 7 chips across 5 rack configurations forming a complete AI factory. The NVL72 rack packs 72 Rubin GPUs + 36 Vera CPUs, delivering up to **35x inference throughput per megawatt**
- **Groq 3 inference accelerator (LPU)**: Purpose-built for the decode phase, using "disaggregated inference" to solve the classic latency-vs-throughput tradeoff
- **Vera CPU as standalone product**: Already "a multi-billion-dollar business for sure," per Huang
- **Feynman roadmap teased**: The post-Rubin Ultra architecture, targeting 2028
- **Nemotron 3 Super** open-source model released at GTC
- Huang projects Blackwell + Vera Rubin to generate **$1 trillion in revenue by 2027**

💡 **The signal**: NVIDIA has conquered training. Now it's coming for inference. The Groq 3 LPU is essentially a death notice for most inference chip startups.

The most dramatic story of the week:

- **Context**: The Trump administration designated Anthropic a "supply chain risk" and ordered all federal agencies to stop using Claude, after Anthropic refused to lift its usage restrictions for military applications (Pentagon called them "woke" policies)

## 🎯 Takeaways

1. **The government AI market is being reshuffled** — Anthropic blacklisted, Google takes the Pentagon, OpenAI gets classified clearance via AWS, Senate approves three tools but not Claude. Regardless of legal outcomes, the short-term power map has changed.

2. **Small models are the real infrastructure play** — GPT-5.4 nano isn't for humans. It's for agents. Large models plan; small models execute. This is the real architecture of agentic AI.

3. **NVIDIA is going from "selling shovels" to "building the mine"** — 7 chips + 5 rack types = a complete AI factory offering. Customers aren't buying individual GPUs anymore; they're buying data center designs. The $1T target is a product roadmap, not a boast.

4. **Anthropic's paradox** — Blacklisted by the government, yet crushing OpenAI 70-30 in enterprise deals. And every one of its competitors is defending it in court — because what happens to Anthropic today could happen to any of them tomorrow.

5. **Apple's quiet bet** — M5 Pro/Max Neural Accelerators put AI compute in every GPU core. A 4x LLM processing speedup signals Apple is dead serious about on-device large model inference.
