## 🏆 Cover Story: OpenAI Closes $110B Round, Signs Pentagon Deal — on the Same Day Anthropic Gets Banned

Last Friday may have been the most consequential single day in AI industry history.

**OpenAI announced a $110 billion funding round** — the largest private capital raise on record. Amazon led with $50B, NVIDIA and SoftBank each contributed $30B, pushing OpenAI's valuation to **$730 billion**. As part of the deal, OpenAI will utilize 2 GW of computing capacity powered by Amazon's Trainium chips. ([Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/27/openai-raises-110b-in-one-of-the-largest-private-funding-rounds-in-history/))

Hours earlier, President Trump **ordered all federal agencies to immediately cease using Anthropic's technology** — after the Pentagon pressured Anthropic to drop restrictions on military use of its AI and Anthropic refused. The GSA promptly removed Anthropic from USAi.gov and the federal procurement schedule. ([Source](https://www.wired.com/story/trump-moves-to-ban-anthropic-from-the-us-government/))

That same afternoon, **OpenAI reached an AI deployment agreement with the Pentagon**, with Sam Altman stating the deal includes "prohibitions on domestic mass surveillance" and "human responsibility for critical decisions." ([Source](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/27/technology/openai-agreement-pentagon-ai.html))

**The bottom line:** Money, power, and values collided in a single Friday afternoon — and the AI industry's relationship with government will never be the same.

## 🔥 Top Stories

### Anthropic Goes All-In on Enterprise Workflows
On Feb 24, Anthropic shipped a **massive Claude Cowork update**: 13 new enterprise plugins, 12 MCP connectors, private plugin marketplaces, and department-specific AI agents for finance, HR, and design. The headline feature: Claude can now **work seamlessly across Excel and PowerPoint** — run an analysis in Excel, generate a presentation in PowerPoint, with full context preservation. ([Source](https://claude.com/blog/cowork-plugins-across-enterprise))

### Samsung Galaxy S26: The First Truly Agentic AI Phone
At Galaxy Unpacked on Feb 25, Samsung launched the S26 series with a triple AI stack: **Gemini 3 + Perplexity + upgraded Bixby**. The breakthrough: Gemini can now **autonomously operate third-party apps** like Uber and DoorDash on your behalf — ordering rides and food through natural language. Circle to Search also upgraded to identify multiple objects in a single image. ([Source](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/25/samsung-s26-launch-gemini-ai-apple-siri.html))

### Google Launches Nano Banana 2
Following the viral success of the original, Google released **Nano Banana 2** on Feb 26 — faster generation, real-time world knowledge integration via Gemini, and built-in SynthID watermarks with C2PA Content Credentials. Now the default image model in Gemini App and AI mode. ([Source](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/ai/nano-banana-2/))

## 🎯 Key Takeaways

1. **AI's politicization is accelerating faster than anyone expected.** Anthropic banned from federal use, OpenAI signs a Pentagon deal — values choices are now directly reshaping competitive landscapes.
2. **Enterprise AI is moving from chat to workflow.** Anthropic's Cowork plugins embed directly into Excel/PPT. This isn't a chatbot — it's an AI co-worker. Microsoft Copilot's moat is under attack.
3. **Phone AI enters the agentic era.** The Galaxy S26's Gemini can autonomously operate third-party apps. The paradigm is shifting from "ask AI" to "let AI act."
4. **The subtext of OpenAI's $110B round:** Amazon, NVIDIA, and SoftBank are forming an alliance against Google's full-stack AI dominance. This isn't an investment — it's a coalition.
5. **Open source is alive.** Zhipu's GLM-5 hit 77.8% on SWE-bench, trained on Huawei Ascend chips, released under MIT. China's chip-independent AI path is producing real results.
