## 🏆 Cover Story: OpenAI's Pentagon Deal Triggers the Biggest Rift in AI

The biggest AI story this week isn't a model launch — it's an industry-defining ethical schism.

OpenAI signed an agreement to deploy its AI models on the Pentagon's classified networks. The fallout was immediate: **Caitlin Kalinowski**, head of OpenAI's robotics team, **resigned in protest**, stating that "guardrails around certain AI uses were not sufficiently defined" before the deal was announced. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-07/openai-s-head-of-robotics-resigns-over-company-s-pentagon-deal))

Meanwhile, Anthropic — which refused to allow Claude for domestic mass surveillance or autonomous weapons — was designated a **"supply chain risk"** by the Pentagon, a label typically reserved for foreign adversaries. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/06/g-s1-112713/pentagon-labels-ai-company-anthropic-a-supply-chain-risk))

The irony: Anthropic's principled stand became a growth engine. Claude gained **over 1 million new users per day** this week, overtaking ChatGPT as the #1 AI app in 20+ countries. Enterprise market share surged from 4% to **40%**, while OpenAI's fell from 50% to 27%. ([Quartz](https://qz.com/anthropic-claude-ai-business-revenue-pentagon-openai-chatgpt))

Ethics, it turns out, is a competitive advantage.

## 🔥 Big Moves This Week

### 1. NVIDIA Invested $40B — Then Said "That's It"
At the Morgan Stanley TMT Conference, Jensen Huang revealed that NVIDIA's **$30B investment in OpenAI** and **$10B in Anthropic** are likely its last. Reason: both companies are headed for IPOs in 2026, after which private investment opportunities close. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/nvidia-will-not-be-able-invest-100-billion-openai-due-ipo-ceo-jensen-says-2026-03-04/))

### 2. Apple, Google, Microsoft Warn the Pentagon
The IT Industry Council (ITIC) — whose members include Apple, Google, Microsoft, and NVIDIA — sent a letter to the DoD warning that the Anthropic supply-chain designation sets a dangerous precedent. Microsoft's legal team concluded that **Anthropic models can remain in Microsoft products**. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/05/microsoft-says-anthropics-products-can-remain-available-to-customers-after-security-risk-designation.html))

### 3. Oracle Cuts 30,000 Jobs
Oracle is laying off 30,000 employees while doubling down on AI data center construction. Larry Ellison is betting the company's future on AI infrastructure to compete with AWS and Azure. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/oracle-plans-thousands-job-cuts-180243222.html))

### 4. Broadcom Targets $100B AI Chip Revenue
Broadcom projects $100B in AI chip revenue by 2027, backed by 6 major clients including Google. Plans to deliver **1 gigawatt of TPUs** for Anthropic in 2026. ([TrendForce](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/03/05/news-broadcom-reportedly-eyes-100b-ai-chip-revenue-in-2027-backed-by-six-key-clients-including-google-meta/))

## 🤖 Model & API Updates

### OpenAI GPT-5.3 Instant
OpenAI acknowledged ChatGPT can be "cringe" and shipped GPT-5.3 Instant. Key changes: reduced moralizing preambles, fewer unsolicited "Stop. Take a breath." responses, and better web search synthesis. Available as `gpt-5.3-chat-latest`. GPT-5.2 Instant retires June 3. ([PCMag](https://www.pcmag.com/news/openai-admits-chatgpt-is-cringe-releases-less-preachy-5-3-model))

### Google Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite
Released March 3 to developers via the Google API. Lightweight model for cost-sensitive workloads. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemini_(language_model)))

### GitHub Deprecating Gemini 3 Pro & GPT-5.1
Both models being retired across all Copilot experiences — Chat, inline edits, agent mode, and code completions. ([GitHub Blog](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-03-02-upcoming-deprecation-of-gemini-3-pro-and-gpt-5-1-models/))

## 🏢 Platform Moves

Analyst Mary Jo Foley reports Microsoft is planning a **365 E7 tier** that bundles Copilot with **Agent 365** — a service for managing and governing AI agents across enterprise environments. Separately, M365 Copilot is adding granular controls over file access during AI processing. ([The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/03/03/microsoft_365_e7_rumors/))

## 🎯 Takeaway

1. **AI ethics is now a market differentiator.** Anthropic got blacklisted by the government for refusing military AI uses — and gained more users in a week than most apps get in a year. OpenAI won the Pentagon contract but lost its robotics lead and public trust. The market is choosing sides.

2. **The IPO countdown has started.** NVIDIA explicitly signaled both OpenAI and Anthropic will go public in 2026. This will fundamentally reshape AI's capital structure and competitive dynamics.

3. **Agents are the next platform battleground.** Microsoft Agent 365, Cursor Automations, Meta AI Shopping — everyone is betting that AI's next phase is autonomous task execution, not just conversation.

4. **The cost of AI infrastructure is reshaping tech.** Oracle is cutting 30K jobs to fund data centers. Broadcom is projecting $100B in chip revenue. The human cost of building AI infrastructure is becoming impossible to ignore.
