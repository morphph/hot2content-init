## 🏆 Cover Story: OpenAI Closes $122B Round at $852B Valuation

The headline writes itself: **OpenAI announced it closed the largest private funding round in history — $122 billion at an $852 billion post-money valuation.** Amazon led with $50B (of which $35B is contingent on IPO or hitting tech milestones), joined by NVIDIA and SoftBank. Retail investors contributed an unusual $3B tranche.

SoftBank [took on $40B in new debt](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/27/why-softbanks-new-40b-loan-points-to-a-2026-openai-ipo/) to cover its $30B commitment — classic Masa. Multiple outlets now point to a **potential OpenAI IPO in H2 2026**.

**Why it matters**: At $852B, OpenAI has crossed from "AI startup" into infrastructure-tier company territory. Meanwhile, Anthropic is approaching $19B in annualized revenue while OpenAI has topped $25B. The arms race is no longer just about models — it's about capital structure, distribution, and whether these companies can justify their valuations through actual business performance.

Sources: [OpenAI blog](https://openai.com/index/accelerating-the-next-phase-ai/) · [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/antoniopequenoiv/2026/03/31/openai-valuation-reaches-852-billion-after-massive-funding-round/) · [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/31/openai-funding-round-ipo.html) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/31/openai-not-yet-public-raises-3b-from-retail-investors-in-monster-122b-fund-raise/)

## 🔥 Top Stories This Week

### 1. Anthropic's "Claude Mythos" Leaked — A "Step Change" Model

A security lapse in Anthropic's CMS exposed details of **"Claude Mythos" (internal codename Capybara)**, an unreleased model sitting above Opus in the product hierarchy. Anthropic confirmed its existence, calling it a "step change" in performance with "dramatically higher" scores in coding, reasoning, and cybersecurity.

**This is not an incremental Opus update — it's a new tier entirely.** Currently in early-access testing only; no public release date announced.

Sources: [Fortune exclusive](https://fortune.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-says-testing-mythos-powerful-new-ai-model-after-data-leak-reveals-its-existence-step-change-in-capabilities/) · [Futurism](https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/anthropic-step-change-new-model-claude-mythos) · [Techzine](https://www.techzine.eu/news/applications/140017/details-leak-on-anthropics-step-change-mythos-model/)

### 2. OpenAI Shuts Down Sora

On March 24, **OpenAI discontinued Sora**, its AI video generation service, citing unsustainable inference costs. From public beta to standalone app to shutdown in roughly six months.

This exposed the fundamental economic challenge of compute-heavy media generation. With an IPO on the horizon, OpenAI is pruning cash-burning product lines. TechCrunch asked the pointed question: is this the start of a broader pullback on AI-generated video?

Sources: [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/mar/24/openai-ai-video-sora) · [NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/25/business/dealbook/sora-openai-finances-ipo.html) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/29/soras-shutdown-could-be-a-reality-check-moment-for-ai-video/)

### 3. Anthropic Wins Injunction Against Trump Administration

A federal judge in San Francisco granted Anthropic a **preliminary injunction**, finding the company "likely to succeed" in its lawsuit against the government's decision to label it a "supply-chain risk." The Pentagon had blacklisted Anthropic after it reportedly refused a Defense Department AI contract on ethical grounds — a move that paradoxically helped Claude reach #1 on the US App Store.

Sources: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-wins-injunction-against-trump-administration-over-defense-department-saga/) · [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-pentagon-dod-claude-court-ruling.html) · [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/26/nx-s1-5762971/judge-temporarily-blocks-anthropic-ban)

## 🎯 Takeaways

1. **Follow the money**: OpenAI's $852B valuation and $122B raise signal unprecedented capital concentration in AI. But Sora's shutdown is the counterpoint — not every AI product finds a sustainable business model, and even the most well-funded company is trimming before going public.

2. **Model competition enters the differentiation era**: March saw 3 frontier models ship in 23 days (GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1, Grok 4.20), plus Claude Mythos and Mistral Small 4. The question is no longer "who's ahead" but "ahead at what" — reliability (GPT), multimodal depth (Gemini), real-time accuracy (Grok), open-source value (Mistral).

3. **Agentic AI goes mainstream**: NVIDIA GTC was entirely framed around agentic deployment. MCP at 97M installs. Google Workspace CLI with AI agent skills. The 2026 keyword isn't "better models" — it's "better agent infrastructure."

4. **Regulation is now a product variable**: Anthropic vs. US Government, EU AI Act's first formal inquiries, Wikipedia's AI content restrictions. Policy uncertainty is becoming a core input to product strategy, not a background concern.

5. **Platform wars enter the data migration phase**: Google's chat import tools signal a shift. The next competitive front isn't just "whose model is better" — it's "who owns the user's data, habits, and memory."
