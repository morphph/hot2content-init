## 🏆 Cover Story: OpenAI Kills Sora, Goes All-In on Enterprise

The biggest shockwave this week: OpenAI officially shut down Sora on March 24.

The shutdown was as dramatic as the product's debut. Disney had been in active talks for a $1 billion character licensing deal. Thirty minutes after a routine partnership meeting, Disney's team was blindsided with the news that Sora was being pulled entirely. Reports indicate Sora was burning ~$15M/day in GPU costs against roughly $2.1M in total lifetime revenue.

Sam Altman framed it as a focus decision: "We need to concentrate compute where it creates long-term value." Translation: Anthropic is winning the enterprise and developer tooling race, and OpenAI needs to close the gap before IPO.

Meanwhile, OpenAI's ads pilot hit $100M in annualized revenue just six weeks after launch, with 600+ advertisers onboarded and self-serve access planned for April. SoftBank secured a staggering $40 billion bridge loan to fund further OpenAI investments.

Killing a consumer product while ramping monetization at speed — OpenAI has officially entered pre-IPO focus mode.

## 🔥 Big Stories This Week

### 1. Anthropic Ships Computer Use + "Mythos" Model Leak

On March 23, Anthropic launched Computer Use in research preview for Claude Cowork and Claude Code. Claude can now directly control your Mac — opening apps, navigating browsers, filling spreadsheets, and running dev tools autonomously. Paired with last week's Dispatch feature (assign tasks from iPhone, come back to finished work on desktop), this is the closest thing to a complete AI desktop agent.

Then came the bombshell: Fortune exclusively reported that an unsecured data store leaked a draft Anthropic blog post revealing **"Claude Mythos"** — described as "by far the most powerful AI model we've ever developed," with a "step change" in reasoning, coding, and cybersecurity. Anthropic confirmed the model exists but said it's still in testing.

### 2. Google's TurboQuant Shakes the Memory Chip Market

Google Research published **TurboQuant**, a compression algorithm that reduces LLM KV-cache memory by 6x with 8x speedup — and zero accuracy loss, no retraining required. It compresses to just 3 bits per value.

The market impact was immediate: memory chip stocks including Micron sold off within hours. Investors had treated "AI needs more memory" as a certainty; TurboQuant is a reminder that software optimization can quickly reshape hardware demand curves. The internet predictably started calling it "real-life Pied Piper."

### 3. Apple Reimagines Siri as a Multi-Model AI Hub

Bloomberg and The Verge report that Apple plans to open Siri to rival AI assistants in iOS 27. Gemini, Claude, and others could plug directly into the iPhone experience instead of staying behind Apple's own model layer.

This is a fundamental strategic pivot: Apple is no longer betting on building one model to rule them all. Instead, the iPhone becomes an **orchestration platform for multiple AI models** — and Apple controls distribution without needing to win the model race.

## 🎯 Takeaway

**This week's theme: Focus.**

OpenAI killed Sora to focus on enterprise. Anthropic doubled down on Computer Use and coding agents. Google used TurboQuant to redefine the efficiency game. Apple chose not to build its own model — it's building the orchestration layer instead.

Key signals to watch:

1. **The competition has shifted from "who has the best model" to "who controls the workflow."** Codex Plugins vs Claude Code vs Gemini CLI — every major player is racing to embed AI into daily tools.

2. **The infrastructure arms race continues — but software can disrupt it overnight.** SoftBank's $40B, Meta's $10B data center, NVIDIA's trillion-dollar pipeline — yet TurboQuant showed that algorithmic breakthroughs can reshape hardware demand in hours.

3. **User migration is real.** The ChatGPT→Claude shift, Google's cross-platform chat import — user loyalty is far more fragile than anyone assumed.

4. **Apple might be the ultimate winner.** If Siri becomes a multi-model switchboard, the iPhone becomes AI's most powerful distribution channel — and Apple doesn't need to train the best model to capture the most value.
