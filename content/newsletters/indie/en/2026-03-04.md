## 🏆 Cover Story: Cursor Hits $2B Annualized Revenue — The AI Coding Supercycle Is Here

Cursor — the four-year-old VS Code fork that started as a developer side tool — just crossed **$2 billion in annualized revenue**, doubling in three months according to Bloomberg.

The growth story is wild: Cursor's original engine was individual devs paying $20/month out of pocket. At $100M ARR, they had roughly 360,000 paying users at an average contract value of $276. Then enterprise kicked in.

Same week, Stripe's 2025 annual letter dropped a bombshell: the number of companies hitting $10M ARR within three months of launch doubled from 2024. Lovable hit $17M ARR in three months. Bolt hit $20M ARR in two months.

**Indie takeaway**: These aren't VC-fueled growth hacks. This is product-led, bottom-up, word-of-mouth growth. Good tools sell themselves.

## 🛠 Build Story: AudioPen — Half-Day Hackathon to $20k MRR

The AudioPen story is a masterclass for indie builders:

- **Origin**: Built during "Half Day Build" hackathon — MVP in hours
- **Stack**: Bubble (web) + Draftbit (mobile) + Xano (backend) — entirely no-code
- **Growth**: Twitter build-in-public + word of mouth
- **Monetization**: Annual and two-year plans only — no monthly option (reduces churn)
- **Challenge**: Survived DDoS attacks that temporarily killed the site

Key insight from Louis: "I would build tools online even if I didn't make money doing it." That mindset is why he stuck around long enough to win.
