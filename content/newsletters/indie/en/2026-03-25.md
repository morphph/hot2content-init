## 🏆 Cover Story: The SaaSpocalypse Is Here — And It's a Signal for Indie Builders

The biggest story this week isn't a launch. It's a crash.

The B2B software ETF (IGV) is down **21%+ year-to-date**, wiping out nearly **$2 trillion** in market cap. Thomson Reuters fell 15.83%. LegalZoom dropped 19.68%. Jefferies downgraded Workday and DocuSign. The press is calling it **"The SaaSpocalypse."**

The trigger? AI agents. When Anthropic released Claude Cowork and Claude Code earlier this year, Wall Street finally internalized what builders already knew: per-seat SaaS pricing is dying. Why pay per human when one AI agent does the work of five?

For indie builders, this is the most interesting moment in years:
- **Opportunity**: Legacy SaaS is getting repriced. AI-native vertical tools have a real window to capture value that's being left on the table.
- **Risk**: If your own product is priced per-seat with no AI-native angle, you're on the wrong side of this trend.

The Drum launched an entire series called "SaaSpocalypse Watch" tracking who's adapting and who's in denial.

## 📊 Trend Signals

1. **Vibe Coding Landscape Is Crystallizing**: Cursor (pro developers) → Lovable (no-code) → Replit (middle ground) → Google Antigravity (free disruptor). The right tool depends on who you are, not which is "best."

2. **"Agent-Native" Is a Real Category Now**: InsForge (agent-native backend), Maestri (agent collaboration canvas), Adaptive (agent computer) — infrastructure is being designed for AI agents, not humans.

3. **SaaSpocalypse = Indie Builder Window**: Legacy SaaS valuations are crashing while AI-native products are scaling faster than ever. If you're building an AI-first vertical tool, the market hasn't been more receptive.

4. **Chinese AI Players Are Moving Fast**: Xiaomi stealth-tested a trillion-parameter model. DeepSeek V4 could drop any day. The open-weight model race is far from over.

5. **Micro-SaaS Acquisitions vs. VC Mania**: On one end, $9B valuations and $400M rounds. On the other, bootstrappers hitting $120K MRR by acquiring and operating small products. Both paths work — completely different games.

The signal this week is unmistakable: **the dividing line between builders and speculators is getting sharper.**
