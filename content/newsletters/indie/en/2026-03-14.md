## 🏆 Cover Story: Google Antigravity Goes From Free to "Please Pay"

The biggest indie dev drama this week: Google's Antigravity agentic coding tool moved from its free preview to a credit-based pricing system ($25 for 2,500 credits). Pro users discovered their quotas no longer reset every few hours — now it's weekly. Mid-project lockouts ensued.

The developer forums lit up. One user on The Register: "They got you hooked for free, now the paywall button is right in your face."

**The lesson for builders:** Free tools always carry pricing risk. When choosing your stack, think about vendor lock-in cost, not just the current price tag.

## 🛠 Build Story: The 2026 Vibe Coding Arms Race

LogRocket dropped a comprehensive [AI Dev Tool Power Rankings](https://blog.logrocket.com/ai-dev-tool-power-rankings/) this week — 18 AI models and 11 dev tools compared across 50+ features. Key takeaways:

**Cursor** shipped Long-running Agents in February. You can now offload tasks to remote machines for 10+ minutes and review from editor, web, or mobile. It's becoming a distributed AI workstation, not just a local IDE.

**Claude Code** doubled down on Auto-Accept Mode and headless/SDK workflows for unattended runs. Strong WCAG compliance and browser compatibility checking.

**OpenAI Codex** added Windows support and GPT-5.4 with 1M token context + native computer use.

**Google Antigravity** nails the AI Studio integration ("Open in Antigravity" goes from prototype to app), but see the pricing controversy above.

**Kimi Code** and **Gemini CLI** remain the only two open-source, self-hostable options.

## 🎯 Takeaway

**This week's core lesson: free lunches always end.**

Google Antigravity's free-to-paid transition is a perfect case study. For indie builders in 2026, the stack selection principles are getting clearer:

- **Self-hostable wins long-term** (Kimi Code, Gemini CLI)
- **Clear pricing beats "free for now"** — you can plan around costs
- **AI-native is a real premium** — 1-3x higher multiples mean the market rewards it
- **Prompts are assets** — manage them with engineering discipline

If you're building a bootstrapped SaaS, the good news: buyers like saas.group are actively acquiring profitable products at $2M-$10M ARR. Build something profitable, and exit options are better than ever.
