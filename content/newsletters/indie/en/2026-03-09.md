## 🏆 Cover Story: Cursor Doubles Revenue in Three Months, Hits $2B ARR

The biggest number this week: **Cursor's annualized revenue topped $2 billion in February**, doubling in just three months. Per Bloomberg, 60% now comes from enterprise customers.

Last November's $2.3B raise (led by Accel and Coatue) valued the company at $29.3B. At the time it felt aggressive. Now? The revenue backs it up.

But there's tension: Anthropic keeps making Claude Code better. Boris Cherny, Claude Code's creator, said he expects the "software engineer" job title to *start disappearing* in 2026. When your upstream provider becomes your competitor, things get interesting.

The broader vibe coding market is on fire too: **Lovable hit $6.6B valuation in under a year. Replit raised at $9B.** AI-assisted coding isn't a demo anymore — it's a massive business.

**Emergent AI: Vibe Code Apps** — an AI-assisted app builder made by twin brothers — climbed to #12 in its category with 30K downloads and **$30K MRR** in just six months.

## 🛠 Build Story

### Jan Luca Sandmann: Bootstrapping AI Computer Agents Without VC

Jan Luca Sandmann wrote a refreshingly honest piece about building [Computer Agents](https://computer-agents.com) — a computer-use agent startup — without venture funding.

His take: In 2026, VC money flows to proven AI plays and pre-IPO giants. Early-stage founders face longer raise cycles and higher traction bars. **But the bootstrap advantage is clear: you only need to impress users, not investors.**

He's building in the same space as Vercept (just acquired by Anthropic for their computer-use capabilities). Competition from BigCo acquisitions is real, but so is the market.
