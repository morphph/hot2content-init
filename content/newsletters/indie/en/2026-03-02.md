## 🏆 Cover Story: Karpathy Says It's "Agentic Engineering" Now

Remember when Karpathy coined "vibe coding" a year ago? This week he moved the goalposts: **the next phase is "agentic engineering."**

His argument: AI-assisted development has graduated from "describe what you want → AI writes code" to "AI agents autonomously execute multi-step engineering tasks"—reading codebases, running tests, fixing bugs, deploying. No vibes. Just engineering.

What this means for indie builders: your competition isn't another solo founder. It's a solo founder commanding a squad of AI agents.

## 💰 Revenue Milestones

### Alexander Van Le: From Failed VC Startup to $20k/mo Bootstrap

Alex co-founded a VC-backed FinTech with four co-founders. Zero revenue. It died. He went back to employment, started posting on X daily, one tweet went viral, he quit his job, and bootstrapped [AI Flow Chat](https://aiflowchat.com/) + [Starpop](https://starpop.ai/). Combined **MRR: $20k**.

Key lesson: **marketing-first product building.** Validate before you code. Use capital for scaling, not survival.

📎 [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/bootstrapping-a-20k-mo-ai-portfolio-after-his-vc-backed-company-failed-rQxwZBD9xWVgfHhIxvbJ)

### Louis Pereira: Half-Day Hackathon → AudioPen → $20k/mo

Louis isn't technical. He built [AudioPen](https://audiopen.ai/) (voice-to-text AI) using Bubble + Draftbit + Xano during a half-day hackathon. Annual-only pricing. Grew to $15k–$20k/mo.

The move: **annual-only subscriptions** = healthier cash flow, stickier users.

📎 [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-an-ai-tool-in-a-half-day-hackathon-and-hitting-20k-mo-BCloDTDRfjuip0pDghap)

## 🛠 Build Story: One Founder + 15 AI Agents = A Company

Aaron Sneed from Florida built **"The Council"**—15 custom GPT agents acting as his Chief of Staff, HR, Legal, Finance, and more. Saves him 20+ hours a week.

Built on Nvidia hardware + OpenAI ChatGPT Business. Way cheaper than hiring. Another solo founder profiled by Business Insider uses a $20/month AI subscription to replace an entire team—vibe-coding her website, compressing 60-minute tasks to one minute.

The solo founder + AI council is becoming a real org structure, not a thought experiment.

## ⚡ Stack Watch

### Cursor Ships Cloud Agents + Computer Use

Cursor ($29.3B valuation) dropped a major update:
- **Cloud Agents**: trigger from Web, Mobile, Slack, or GitHub
- **Agent Computer Use**: AI agents control their own VMs—build, test, verify autonomously
- Directly competing with Anthropic's Claude Code, OpenAI, Microsoft

For indie devs: your AI coding assistant can now spin up in the cloud and work while you sleep.

📎 [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/24/cursor-announces-major-update-as-ai-coding-agent-battle-heats-up.html) | [Apidog](https://apidog.com/blog/cursor-agent-computer-use/)

### Perplexity Computer: A Multi-Model Digital Worker

Perplexity launched **Computer**—orchestrating 19 AI models (Claude Opus, Gemini, Grok) to execute multi-step projects. Not just answering questions. Full workflows: market research, coding, data analysis.

The signal: multi-agent orchestration is moving from frameworks to consumer products.

📎 [PCWorld](https://www.pcworld.com/article/3071595/perplexitys-new-tool-deploys-teams-of-ai-agents.html) | [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/technology/tech-news/aravind-srinivas-perplexity-computer-ai-digital-worker-projects-details-126022600193_1.html)

### MiniMax M2.5: Open-Weight Challenger

Shanghai's MiniMax dropped M2.5 right after their Hong Kong IPO. Open-weight, benchmarking near Claude Opus 4.6, significantly cheaper. If you're building AI wrappers, this is another option to slash model costs.
